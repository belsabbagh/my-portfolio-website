I'm human, and with that comes the urge to vent my thoughts and feelings, and I
have a lot of those now that I'm looking to start off my career. So, I'm making
my own blog to let those kinds of feelings out. I already spend so much time of
my day using and thinking about software, and since I want my website to reflect
as much of me as possible, this sounds like a great idea.

I've recently finished my mandatory military service, which delayed me a whole
year from getting a job and keeping up with the many changes in the AI and
software industry. A lot has happened in the past year alone, from newly
introduced industry standards, and even breaking changes in tools and frameworks
I've been using, and it's been embarrasingly disheartening to see all the things
I missed out on. "It's never too late to start," they say, so I thought I'd do
something different this time and keep a human record of my work and progress
outside of just posting achievements on LinkedIn. Progress is more failure than
success anyway.
